To Show Up

2024

There was a time after I decided to step away from UP Diliman's Architecture course and stayed at a friend’s place for a while. During that period, we spent a lot of time talking, and one conversation in particular left a lasting impression on me. My friend reminded me of the importance of perseverance, of consistently showing up, even when the weight of tasks, expectations, or self-doubt feels overwhelming. He shared that professors, too, can be understanding and supportive if they see genuine effort and commitment.

This simple yet profound lesson reminded me that progress is built not only on talent or intelligence but also on persistence and presence. By continuing to show up, I open the door to learning, growth, and opportunities for guidance that I might otherwise miss. That conversation helped me realize that stepping away may offer temporary relief, but facing challenges, however daunting, ultimately shapes resilience and self-discipline.

Since then, I’ve tried to carry that lesson with me: to show up, to engage, and to trust that my effort, even in small doses, is seen and valued.
